# Welcome — why I started this blog

Hi, and thanks for stopping by! 👋

I've wanted a small corner of the internet that's *mine* — a place to keep notes
on the things I read, the projects I tinker with, and the ups and downs of
research life. This is it.

## What you'll find here

Each week (that's the goal, anyway) I'll post something short:

- **Paper notes** — a quick summary of an interesting paper I'm reading, in plain language.
- **Project logs** — what I'm building, what broke, and what I learned.
- **Research life** — the human side of a PhD: failures, small wins, and lessons.

## A bit about my work

I work at the intersection of **plant breeding**, **phenomics**, and
**artificial intelligence**. In practice that means I spend my time:

1. Flying drones over breeding trials to measure plants at scale.
2. Building models that predict how genotypes will perform across environments.
3. Writing a lot of Python and R to glue it all together.

> The best way to learn something is to explain it to someone else.
> That's really what this blog is for.

If any of that sounds interesting, stick around — and feel free to
[reach out](https://www.linkedin.com/in/swaskaushal/). I love talking shop.

— Swas
